Kishore Kumar (1929–1987)

Singer • Actor • Composer • Filmmaker — Celebrated playback singer of Indian cinema, known for versatility and emotional range.

Biography

Kishore Kumar, born Abhas Kumar Ganguly on August 4, 1929, was a multifaceted artist—renowned as one of India's greatest playback singers and a charismatic film personality. Over four decades he sang in multiple languages and delivered a vast repertoire ranging from playful yodelling to soulful ballads.

Career highlights

  • Sang thousands of songs for Hindi cinema; collaborated with top music directors like R.D. Burman.
  • Worked as actor, director, producer and screenwriter.
  • Known for his unique voice, comedic timing, expressive singing and ability to write and compose.

Selected quotes

"Music should be like a river — alive, changing, and always flowing."

Quick facts

  • Born: 4 Aug 1929
  • Died: 13 Oct 1987
  • Occupations: Singer, actor, composer, producer
  • Languages: Hindi, Bengali, Marathi, Kannada and more

Notable songs

  • "Mere Sapno Ki Rani"
  • "Roop Tera Mastana"
  • "Ek Ladki Bheegi Bhaagi Si"
  • "Zindagi Ek Safar Hai Suhana"
  • "Yeh Jo Mohabbat Hai"

Timeline

  • 1940s–50s: Early career in Bombay movies; performed in Bengali and Hindi films.
  • 1960s: Rose to prominence as a leading playback singer; notable collaborations with top composers.
  • 1970s–80s: Peak popularity; iconic songs, experimentation with genres, and continued film work.
  • 1987: Passed away, leaving a lasting legacy in Indian music and cinema.
